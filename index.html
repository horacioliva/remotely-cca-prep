Exam day reality

What they don't tell you in the official guide

Practical lessons pulled directly from Remoters who already cleared CCAF. Read this before you register — it'll change how you prep.

Score 900/1000 on the practice exam first

Anthropic's recommendation, confirmed by every remoter we talked to. The real exam is more complex than the practice. Don't book your slot until you're consistently clearing 900.

Take it within days of finishing the courses

Material fades fast. Several remoters waited two-to-three weeks after the Foundational Track and regretted it. Schedule the exam while concepts are still sharp.

One attempt. Six months to retake.

Fail and you wait six months before you can sit it again. This is not a "try it and see" credential — prepare seriously, or wait until you're ready.

Put a UPS on your router

The session is proctored with camera and mic on. A power outage or dropped internet during the exam can fail you automatically. One of our remoters survived an outage because of a UPS — yours might not.

Hands-on beats reading

The hardest questions are scenario-based. Wire up an MCP server. Build a multi-agent loop. Write five tool descriptions. The exam tests what you'd do, not what you've memorized.

Results in days, not weeks

Anthropic publishes a 7-business-day window. In practice, scores often come back in 2-3 days, with a section-by-section breakdown so you know exactly where you landed.
